## 2. Gene-level description of the baseline substrain difference

Of 19,662 genes tested at baseline, 103 (0.5%) reach FDR<0.05, and 64 (0.3%)
reach the stricter threshold of FDR<0.05 and |log2FC|>1
(`tables/TableS_top_baseline_genes.csv`). The three genes specifically raised
by the reviewer are among the most significant in the entire dataset:
Tmem215 (log2FC=+3.39, FDR=5.2x10^-18, higher in ByJcl), Glp1r (log2FC=+3.01,
FDR=1.3x10^-15, higher in ByJcl), and Nlrp1b (log2FC=-3.45, FDR=3.5x10^-6,
higher in AJcl). We replace the previous "modest" wording with this explicit
count and these three named, quantified examples: the baseline substrain
difference is real and detectable at a modest fraction of loci, several of
them at very high significance, while core podocyte-identity markers (Wt1,
Nphs1) are unaffected (below, item 3).

## 3. Wt1/Nphs1 robustness check

At baseline, both markers are clearly non-significant (Wt1: log2FC=+0.14,
FDR=0.87; Nphs1: log2FC=+0.23, FDR=0.79), directly supporting the manuscript's
claim of no baseline difference in podocyte identity between substrains. At
Day 5 post-ADR, the picture is more nuanced than "not robustly changed"
implies: Wt1 is borderline (log2FC=-0.71, FDR=0.050) and Nphs1 is
non-significant but closer to the threshold (log2FC=-0.78, FDR=0.064). We
recommend describing this as a small, borderline reduction rather than an
absence of change, and the volcano labels make this checkable at a glance
rather than relying on prose alone.

## 5. Concordance of the ADR response between substrains

Genome-wide, the ADR response (ADR vs Ctrl log2FC) is significantly
correlated between substrains (Pearson r=0.535, 95% CI 0.525-0.545; Spearman
rho=0.425; n=19,662 genes; `figures/FigS_ADR_response_concordance.png`).
Direction concordance rises with signal strength: 62.8% genome-wide, 87.0%
among genes significant in either substrain, and 99.6% among the 257 genes
significant in both. Only 24/19,662 genes show a significant
substrain-by-treatment interaction term. We conclude that the two substrains
mount an ADR response that is highly concordant in direction and of broadly
similar magnitude (OLS slope=0.62 relative to the y=x identity line), with the
caveat that A_ADR_vs_Ctrl (n=2 vs 3, A-ADR1 excluded as the flagged low-purity
sample) has less statistical power than B_ADR_vs_Ctrl (n=3 vs 3), so the
larger raw DEG count in B (2,674 vs 298 genes at FDR<0.05) partly reflects
this power asymmetry rather than a larger true biological response -- the
genome-wide correlation and the interaction test, not the raw DEG counts, are
the appropriate summaries for whether the response differs between
substrains.
